# ACOUSTIC SIDE CHANNELS · ASC

> A sphere of **UD0** — the ROOT0 universe / biosphere. Domain: **HERMES**.

ACOUSTIC SIDE CHANNELS (ASC) — Paper III of VI in the AUDIO CIPHER CHANNELS series, and the INVERSION of the rest: no sender at all. A machine's incidental sound is correlated with its internal state (which key you struck, which bit the cipher multiplied), so the secret — most often the KEY — escapes on its own; the attacker only listens. The lineage (ENGULF 1956 counted a Hagelin cipher-machine's rotor clicks through a bugged phone → Asonov–Agrawal 2004 keyboard emanations → Backes 2010 printer-sound→text → Genkin–Shamir–Tromer 2013–14 pulled a full 4096-bit RSA key from a laptop's coil whine, mic up to 4 m → deep-learning typing-over-a-call 2023). Live: type a phrase and a Goertzel-bank eavesdropper that never saw the keyboard recovers it from the SOUND alone (verified 5/5 phrases + 30/30 random strings). ⚑ The exact opposite of a watermark — extraction not insertion: if your signing KEY can be lifted from the whine of the laptop that holds it, every anchored hash collapses at the root; the defense is to BREAK the correlation (masking, shielding, cryptographic blinding). Two-layer honest: lineage real; the demo gives each key a clean separated fingerprint so the principle is legible — real keyboards leak messy overlapping ones (why the field needed ML to reach ~90%). Plays as the keystroke-leak layer of [[the-procession]]; sibling to [[cipher-steganography]] / [[cipher-ultrasonic]].
